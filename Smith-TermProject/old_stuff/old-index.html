Considerations When Identifying Birds

The main things to think about when identifying a bird are what the bird looks like, what the bird sounds like, what the bird is doing, and lastly the location of the bird. This location refers to its current location as well as where it is typically found.

Sight

Look for distinguising characterisics such as primary colors, markings on the body, head shape, and eye color. When you visit the pages for each of the birds, there will be more details.

A beautiful bird that is fun to see this time of year, and easy to spot based on its primary color, is the Western Bluebird.

Sound

It is important to not just listen but to hear. The difference between sounds can be very subtle.

Behavior

This is often under-emphasized in bird watching, but is vitally important when you have birds that look very similar from a distance.

Environment and/or Location

This is often useful for helping you rule out birds. For example, if you are in Western Oregon, it is rare to see a Cassin's Finch and it is more likely that you are seeing a House Finch or possibly a Purple Finch. If you were in Eastern Oregon though, it would not be uncommon and you would need to rely on other features such as bill shape and tail length.

Birds to Begin Identifying

It is easiest to start identify birds that are in your backyard such as sparrows, chickadees, and finches. Head over to the Backyard Birds page to learn more about common backyard birds in the Portland-Metro area.
